What are cookies?

Cookies are text files that are downloaded to users’ terminal equipment (computer, tablet, mobile telephone, etc.) and saved in their browser’s memory. It is made in order to store data that can be updated and retrieved by the entity responsible for its installation. These cookies can not read data off your hard disk.

What is Local Storage?

Local Storage is a technology similar to cookies. The software element based on this technology is downloaded to the user's terminal equipment (computer, tablet, mobile telephone, etc.) and saved in his/her browser's memory, as cookies are. The difference is that more information is saved in the user's terminal equipment than in case of cookies. Local Storage elements cannot read data on your hard disk.
